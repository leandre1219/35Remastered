Learning ISO

What exactly is ISO?

ISO is the amount of sensitivity your camera has to light. A lower ISO number means it is less sensitive to light, the higher the ISO number the higher the sensitivity. In simpler terms a lower ISO will capture less light, a higher ISO will capture more light. ISO usually starts at 100 and goes to around 3000. Depending on the camera and film you buy they may have different numbers.

How to use ISO

When taking photos using film ISO, Aperture and shutter speed all work together to create a beautiful photo. Using ISO can be a hard concept to understand at first but it is very much worth understanding. Here are some ways you can use ISO to improve your photos.

Low Light Situations

  • Use the lowest (base) ISO your camera has when taking photos with a large source of light.
  • If you have a high ISO in a well lit area it will most likely ruin the quality of the photo and make it very grainy or just not appealing.

Well Lit Area

  • Use a higher ISO when the area is not well lit.
  • When raising the ISO you are telling your camera to allow in more light.
  • A higher ISO may also be needed when you need to get action shots.
  • It can be very beneficial if you are shooting a fast motion shot for example birds or any sort of animal.
  • Some raise the ISO when capturing the night sky to help with the moving stars.
