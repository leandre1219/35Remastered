Using Aperture

What is Aperture?

Aperture and shutter speed go hand in hand together. Depending on the shutter speed you set the aperture changes the size of the hole to allow or not allow lights. Aperture allows in more or less light for exposure and controls sharpness. Reducing the aperture size is often called “stopping down”, while increasing the size is referred to as “opening up.”

How to use Aperture

Aperture is measured in “F stops.” Depending on your camera it starts with f/1 - f/20. The larger the number, F20 for instance means it has a smaller opening. A smaller F number means the hole is more open and allows in more light. Hopefully this page will help you learn how to use it.

When to use a low aperture (F) number

  • Use a lower aperture when a lot of light is available
  • Trying to obtain a shallow depth of field
  • When using a lower F number you should be further away from your subject

When to use a high aperture (F) number

  • Use the highest aperture available when taking photos in very low light situations
  • Trying to obtain a large depth of field
  • A high aperture allows you to take photos without using a flash
  • High speed action shots require high aperture
  • Be aware fast action may require more aperture
  • Large apertures don't allow you to change shutter speed
